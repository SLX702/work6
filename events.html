The Three Manly Games

Experience the thrilling traditional competitions at the heart of Nadam Festival

Horse Racing

The horse racing competition is one of the most spectacular events at the Nadam Festival. Unlike Western horse racing, Mongolian races are long-distance cross-country events, usually covering 15-30 kilometers, depending on the age of the horses.

Unique Features

What makes Mongolian horse racing unique is that the jockeys are children aged 5 to 13. Mongolians believe that children make the lightest and best jockeys. The horses are not ridden with saddles, and the young jockeys wear colorful traditional costumes.

Event Details

  • Distance: 15-30 km depending on horse age
  • Jockeys: Children aged 5-13
  • Horses: Mongolian native breeds only
  • Categories: Divided by horse age (2-year-olds, 3-year-olds, etc.)
  • Prize: The top five horses are awarded titles

Wrestling

Mongolian wrestling, known as "Bökh," is one of the oldest sports in Mongolia and a central event at the Nadam Festival. Unlike other wrestling styles, there are no weight classes and the matches are single-elimination.

Traditional Rules

Wrestlers wear distinctive costumes: a tight shoulder vest (zodog), shorts (shuudag), and Mongolian boots. The goal is to get your opponent to touch the ground with any part of their body above the knee. There are hundreds of techniques passed down through generations.

Event Details

  • Participants: 512 or 1024 wrestlers in elimination matches
  • Duration: Continues until only one wrestler remains
  • Costumes: Traditional zodog and shuudag required
  • Victory Dance: Winners perform the "eagle dance" after each match
  • Titles: Winners earn traditional titles like "Falcon" or "Elephant"

Archery

Mongolian archery has a history dating back to the time of Genghis Khan, when skilled archers were essential for hunting and warfare. Today, it remains an important part of the Nadam Festival, with both men and women competing.

Competition Style

Archers use traditional Mongolian composite bows made from wood, horn, and sinew. Targets are small leather cylinders placed on the ground at distances of 75 meters for men and 60 meters for women. Teams take turns shooting arrows while judges closely observe.

Other Cultural Events

Ankle Bone Shooting

A traditional game where players flick sheep ankle bones at a target, testing precision and skill.

Folk Dance & Music

Traditional performances featuring the morin khuur (horsehead fiddle) and khoomei (throat singing).

Traditional Crafts

Demonstrations of Mongolian craftsmanship including felt-making, embroidery, and metalwork.

Food Festival

Sample traditional Mongolian cuisine like khorkhog (barbecue), buuz (dumplings), and airag (fermented mare's milk).

Costume Parade

A colorful display of traditional Mongolian clothing from different regions and ethnic groups.

Opening Ceremony

A grand spectacle featuring hundreds of performers showcasing Mongolian history and culture.

About Nadam

The Nadam Festival is celebrated annually in Inner Mongolia, usually in July or August.
